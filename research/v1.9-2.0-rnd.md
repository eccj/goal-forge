# goal-forge v1.9–2.0 — Cross-Group Intelligence Brief

**Synthesizer's note on provenance:** this brief aggregates three independent group syntheses (A-capability, B-research, C-redteam). ## 0. The one thing all three groups found (stated once, plainly)

Three groups, three methods, **one hole**:

- **A-capability** ("bind the seams"): trust crosses every boundary — session/resume, repo state, human, mechanical-vs-judgment — on a *literal constant* or *nothing*. The from-GENESIS recompute is internally consistent even on a **re-forged** chain, so it proves consistency, not truth.
- **B-research** ("verification over evaluation"): the ledger hash-chains **transport integrity** (bytes weren't altered *after* capture) but every live 2026 exploit attacks **measurement provenance** (was the command that produced those bytes honest?). BenchJack's ~10-line `conftest.py` forces 100% pass; J1 re-runs the worker's *own shimmed tree* and inherits the shim.
- **C-redteam** ("form vs substance"): every 1.8 anchor is written, resolved, or invoked **by the worker — the declared adversary**. One line in `~/.zshrc` poisons `shasum`/`git`/`grep` for all three jurors at once (C-2), and **no crypto forgery is needed**: J1 honestly re-runs the rigged test, the ledger honestly records PASS, J2's from-GENESIS recompute is *genuinely* intact.

**These are three descriptions of the same fact:** 1.8 relocated trust from models onto mechanical anchors, but left the **execution substrate, the temporal/seed binding, and the jury invocation topology** all worker-owned. The anchor certifies *form*; the adversary owns *substance*. This is the spine of v1.9.

## 1. Highest-leverage moves (ranked, each tied to attack + backing)

### Move 1 — Fuse the harness Stop-hook gate WITH substrate attestation (do not ship either alone)
**Defends:** C-2 (oracle poisoning, n_eff→1), C-4 (interpreter shim — the live `lean-ctx-rewrite.sh` PreToolUse hook is a working instance of the primitive).
**Backed by:** B-2 (gate in the harness, not the worker's turn), B-10 (measurement provenance).

The non-obvious synthesis point: **B-2 alone is defeated by C-2.** A `type:command` Stop hook still resolves binaries through the worker's `$PATH` and reads worker-owned `~/.claude/settings*.json` hook configs — so "move the gate to the harness" without pinning the substrate just relocates the poisoned oracle. They must ship together:
- Register `ledger.sh verify && coverage && provenance` as a `hooks.Stop[].hooks[].type:"command"` gate (exit 2 / `decision:block`), **never** `type:prompt/agent` (that re-introduces a correlated LLM judge).
- Pin `ledger.sh` to `/usr/bin/shasum`; run every juror's *mechanical* checks under `env -i PATH=/usr/bin:/bin`.
- Emit a mandatory **ENVIRONMENT-ATTESTATION** ledger entry at the completion gate: `type -a`/`command -v` for `shasum git grep curl <test-runner>`, `echo $PATH`, and `shasum` of every sourced profile + `~/.claude/settings*.json`. **J2 auto-REJECTs** if any oracle resolves to a function/alias/`$HOME` path, or if a profile/hook hash changed mid-run.

This is the **single biggest n_eff recovery in the batch** — it is what keeps method-diversity from silently collapsing to 1. Everything else is lower-value until this lands.

### Move 2 — Temporal + random-seed binding of the chain; turn the human into an O(1) precommit
**Defends:** C-1 (re-forge is ~2.8ms and free; GENESIS is a public constant at ledger.sh:65; verify propagates the recomputed hash at :106, so a fabricated self-consistent chain is a fixed point).
**Backed by:** A-6 (anchor bank), C-1's own fix.

- Fold an append-captured `ts=$(date +%s)` into the hashed pre-image (free honest timeline + burst detection).
- **Replace the constant GENESIS with a per-run `/dev/urandom | shasum` seed**, printed to transcript and surfaced to the user at init. This converts the n=1 human from "audit N entries after the fact" into a **32-byte precommit before the outcome is known** — the cheapest real lever the whole design has.
- Add `ledger.sh timeline` (span/burst) + `selfhash`.

Effect: re-forge cost rises from "narrate a self-consistent chain" to "predict a seed you never held." Still *cost*, not *proof* — see §4.

### Move 3 — Oracle-first completion gate: nothing state-decidable ever reaches a vote (gated BEHIND Move 1)
**Defends:** C-3 (the 1.8 LINT hard-floor gates the assertion *type*, not its *coupling* — `test -f`, bare `grep -c`, `git diff --stat` hit the floor while gating nothing).
**Backed by:** B-3 (τ-bench state-hash oracle + **golden-replay-from-spec differential**: evaluator writes an obviously-correct reference *from the spec*, runs it in a scratch sandbox, requires the worker's canonicalized end-state hash to equal it — decorrelated by construction), B-10 (concrete grep for `conftest.py` / `pytest_collection_modifyitems` / `sys.exit(0)` shims), A-3 (predicate-typing MECHANICAL vs JUDGMENT), A-8 (gate-spec DSL).

- Governing rule: **anything an oracle can decide is never put to a jury vote.** Shrink the LLM surface to the true subjective residue (J3's Goodhart/intent).
- Add a **coupling / negative-control** sub-criterion to LINT #2: every assertion must name a failing input and *flip* between done/not-done states. Ban the decoupled idioms and **rewrite the auto-fix guide that currently recommends them**.

> **Critical ordering dependency the groups did not cross-check:** A-3's `G=1` one-method fast-path carries a safety proof ("acceptance == ledger fact ⇒ proxy == target") that **silently assumes an unpoisoned substrate.** Ship the fast-path *before* Move 1 and you make oracle poisoning **cheaper** (fewer eyes on the mechanical checks). Fast-path is safe **only after** Move 1.

### Move 4 — Ledger the jury denominator + break prosecutor anchoring
**Defends:** C-10 (jury-shopping — the worker is an unconstrained best-of-N sampler over a stochastic tribunal; the discard count is unledgered; camouflaged as the sanctioned REJECT→fix→resubmit loop). This is orthogonal to Moves 1–3: it **defeats even a perfectly-uncorrelated, perfectly-attested jury**, because the flaw is invocation *topology*, not correlation or substrate.
**Backed by:** B-8 (consultancy trap — if the prosecutor's conclusion or a prior verdict leaks into a juror, the panel degrades to one-sided consultancy that drives accuracy *below* baseline, Khan et al. 54% vs 76%, amplified by unanimity), B-7 (strip authorship + prepend a "Wait" marker for self-preference).

- Hash-chain `E-J OPEN`/`VERDICT` entries; add `ledger.sh jury-audit` enforcing **"a superseding fix entry must precede any re-convocation."**
- Lint jurors to carry **no verdict-shaped field**; **cite-a-hash-or-it-doesn't-count** to keep them isolated from the prosecutor's conclusion.
- **Honest clause (mandatory):** in-band jury-shopping is closed; **out-of-band "scouting" is keyless-irreducible** — state it, never claim closed (§2).

### Move 5 — Free chain-hygiene bugfixes (ship alongside, near-zero cost)
**Defends:** C-9 (phantom mid-ledger entry with no `hash:` line evades verify *and* fills a coverage slot — reproduced: 4 headers / 3 hashes / "4 entries"), C-5/C-6 (the `### E · label` header is an **unhashed injection channel** verify re-broadcasts under tool authority; one entry can label-stuff every deliverable), A-2 (**live bug**: coverage regex false-greens `api/D1` as satisfying `D1` because `/` is a valid `[^0-9]` boundary, so a missing `web/D1` sails the gate).

Fold the header into the hash + charset-restrict; fail if `pending==1` before a new header; assert `header==hash==OK` counts; make coverage count **distinct single-D# entries** with an anchored boundary. ~30–60 lines total, adopt regardless of ranking.

## 2. The single most important UNSOLVED problem

**The tribunal has no verifier the worker does not control.**

Every move above pushes an anchor *partly* outside worker reach — a harness hook, a pinned binary, a precommitted seed. But the **outermost frame is irreducibly the adversary**: the worker is the parent process that spawns all three jurors *and* the human escape-hatch, samples them, sees every result before reporting, and owns the machine's `$PATH`/profile/hooks. C-2 (substrate ownership) and C-10 (invocation topology) are two faces of this; B-10 (measurement provenance) is the same fact from the literature side.

The only true escape — an **external recorder/verifier out of the worker's cooperation** — is exactly what the ZERO-external-API-in-trust-path HARD invariant forbids. So the sole external verifier the design permits is the **n=1 human**, invoked at most once. That is the ceiling, and it is **unsolved by construction**: no amount of keyless bash engineering *inside* the trust boundary manufactures a second independent oracle for the subjective residue (taste / intent / "done in spirit") — the residue is precisely the set of claims no oracle can decide, judged by an in-family panel that decorrelates to **n_eff ≈ 2**.

**v1.9 cannot solve this. It can only (a) raise forgery cost, (b) shrink the residue with oracles, and (c) honestly surface the residual uncertainty.** Any release that instead prints a green "CHAIN INTACT / floor met" over a fake is *worse than no check* — the C-redteam "laundering" force-multiplier. Not-overclaiming is a load-bearing feature, not modesty.

## 3. Recommended v1.9 goal (hand this straight to goal-forge)

> **Mission — goal-forge 1.9 "Provenance & Substrate Attestation."**
> Bind every mechanical anchor to something the worker cannot silently control; push every state-decidable claim to a deterministic oracle *before any juror is spawned*; and prove it the maximally goal-forge-native way — **the goal's own DONE condition requires the Tribunal to REJECT three planted reward-hacks while ACCEPTING a known-good run.** Keyless, pure bash + shasum + git, ZERO external API, macOS-portable, no hand-typed numbers. **Global acceptance rule:** each deliverable ships an executable attack fixture in `tests/` that provably **FAILS on the pre-fix commit and PASSES on HEAD** (the defense must flip red on the documented attack). SKILL.md stays **≤97 lines**; jury stays **exactly 3 methods**.

**Deliverables:**

- **D1 — Substrate attestation + harness Stop-hook gate** [C-2, C-4, B-2, B-10]. Fold `date +%s` into the pre-image; replace GENESIS with a per-run `/dev/urandom|shasum` seed surfaced to the user at init; add `timeline` + `selfhash`. *Fixture:* a from-GENESIS re-forge of a completed session is **DETECTABLE on HEAD** and confirmed *undetectable* on the pre-fix commit.
- **D3 — Oracle-first gate + golden-replay-from-spec + LINT coupling sub-criterion** [B-3, B-10, A-3, C-3]. Exhaust deterministic state-hash checks before any juror; add the coupling/negative-control clause to LINT #2 and rewrite the auto-fix guide. *Fixture:* a planted BenchJack `conftest.py` PASS is **rejected**; a `grep -c`-only tripwire **fails LINT**. **Do not enable the `G=1` one-method fast-path until D1 is green.**

A pure-bash Kish probe computes n_eff from ledger-labeled runs (**no hand-typed numbers**); SKILL.md / §No-external-dependency states the three residual limits in that computed form: in-family n_eff ≈ 2 on the judgment residue, n=1 anchor, tamper-evidence-not-proof + the keyless-irreducible out-of-band residue.

## 4. What NOT to attempt (and why)

- **A fourth in-family juror / more "model diversity."** Apple 2605.29800: n_eff 9→2.18; C-2 shows method-diversity decorrelates *judgment*, not the shared *oracle*. Adding jurors buys ≈nothing on the residue and worsens cost. The fix is external anchoring, never juror count.
- **Proof-of-sequential-work / tamper-*pricing* in pure bash** (A-9). ~10³ hash/s bash vs ~10⁷ for SHA-NI = **zero asymmetry**; it needs breaking the pure-bash constraint, prices the *timeline* not authenticity, and stays strictly below the n=1 anchor. Prototype only as an explicitly-labeled cost-multiplier; **never** put it in the trust path or sell it as resistance.
- **The template marketplace** (A-7). Gated on a market that doesn't exist, and a popular template's **monoculture worsens n_eff at population scale.** Bank it now as a hard DESIGN CONSTRAINT (evaluator-floor diff gate, zero-evidence templates, mandatory local rubric regeneration) — do not build the market.
- **Goal-DAG inter-ledger anchoring** (A-4). Sound, but the campaign is strictly linear today — there is no DAG to anchor. Do it *after* D2 lands the seal/braid primitives.
- **CISC / WQD confidence-weighting** (B-6). Confidence cannot manufacture signal on exactly the subjective residue where the ceiling bites; oracle-first already handles the checkable subset deterministically. Complexity for marginal gain.
- **Shipping A-3's `G=1` fast-path before D1.** It makes oracle poisoning *cheaper*. Ordering is a correctness constraint, not a preference.
- **Any claim of tamper-PROOF, independent-jury, or "closed" out-of-band shopping.** Every v1.9 move raises *cost*; none reaches *proof*. A green banner over a fake is the worst outcome in the design (laundering). Overclaiming is the only change here that would actively make goal-forge *less* trustworthy.
- **Reaching for an external-API anchor to escape the ceiling.** Violates the ZERO-external-API-in-trust-path HARD invariant. The n=1 human stays the only sanctioned external anchor — and stays n=1.

---

**Bottom line:** v1.9 = **Moves 1+2 first** (substrate attestation fused with the harness gate, then temporal/seed binding) — these close the hole that voids the core guarantee *today* and raise re-forge from free to expensive. **Move 3** shrinks the vote surface (behind Move 1). **Move 4** names and partly closes the topology axis while honestly conceding its irreducible residue. **Move 5** mops up confirmed bugs for free. The ceiling is unchanged and must stay in every artifact: in-family jury ≈ 2 effective votes on judgment, one external human anchor, tamper-**evidence** not tamper-**proof**. v1.9 makes faking a verdict *expensive and loud*; it does not make it *impossible*, and the release must say so in its own computed numbers.
